BattleMech Building

There are so many approaches to 'Mech building. You will find that each custom 'Mech has its own personality, just as will yours. But as I say "Listen to the experts, then do what you were going to do in the first place." So, here are the experts:

  • Expert 'Mech building tips from the Battle Mech Training Academy.
  • The 'Techs at the Gaming World, AKA Solaris 7, have something more to add.

And here are some recommendations that you may not find in your 'Mech's technical manuals::

  • Do not over-use locking missiles. Your targeting skills will only get better if you practice targeting, not locking. Locking missiles are good for long range volleys while you are marching into medium range. Once there, unload with your auto-cannons, PPCs, and medium lasers.
  • Keep at least one energy weapon in the torso or the head. This will probably be your last standing weapon after all others have been destroyed.
  • Keep ammunition in the side torsos and/or arms. Keep that ammo on the same side as the weapon that it is supplying. This organization helps when the ammo is ignited and the entire half of the 'Mech disintegrates in a white flash. You want an SSRM6 Ammo (left torso) explosion to take out the SSRM6 (left torso), instead of the ER-PPC (right torso).
  • Keep heat-sinks and jump jets in the feet and torso of the BattleMech. Preserve both as long as possible. The heat-dicipating heat sinks allow more frequnt use of your energy weapons and the jump jets are good tools for avoiding direct hits.
  • Increase your 'Mech's armor at least a reasonable tonnage. You will need this protection on the battlefield.
  • Put all Endo-Steel and Ferro-Fibrous armor components in the arms. In the current simulators (MechWarrior2 series), your armor does not have to be distributed. Beware though, MechWarrior3 will probably require the 'Mech builder to have the Endo-Steel and Ferro-Fibrous armor distributed evenly throughout the 'Mech...so do not go getting used to this method of freeing up critical slots.
